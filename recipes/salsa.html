Salsa

Description

This recipe is as close as I can remember to a salsa recipe I was taught ages ago by my aunt. The fire roasted tomatoes are really what give it a great taste. Some things have been tweaked to my liking, but overall its really not that hard to make a good salsa. This is best paired with a really good chip, don't skimp out on that aspect.

In my opinion, the smoking adds a lot of flavor, but honestly if you really wanted to, you could leave it out. Another option is to roast the onions and peppers on a stovetop, grill, or oven before hand. That would still give it that smokey flavor.

Ingredients

  • 14 oz Fire Roasted Diced Tomatoes
  • 2 bunch Green Onions
  • 2-3 Jalepeno (or other pepper)
  • 1 bunch Cilantro
  • 3-5 Cloves of Garlic
  • Lime (to taste)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Tortilla Chips, for serving

Preperation

  1. Chop green onion, jalapeno, cilantro, and garlic into large chunks
  2. Combine all ingredients in a large blender (or work in multiple batches)
  3. Blend together until is it a slightly chunky consistency
  4. Taste, add more salt or lime if needed, and serve

Optional

Before blending, use a smoking cloche on ingredients to add a nice smokey flavor
